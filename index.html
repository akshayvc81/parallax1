वाराणसी

The city gets its name from two rivers — Varuna and Assi — which meet here. The word Kashi is derived from the word ‘Kas’ which means to shine. Varanasi is famous for the bathing ghats along the banks of river Ganga. Pilgrims throng these ghats to take a holy dip to wash themselves of their sins. Varanasi has been synonymous with the majestic river Ganga and its numerous rivulets.

Ganga Aarti

The Ganga Aarti is a daily evening ritual that takes place at various ghats along the Ganges River in Varanasi, India. The ceremony is a visual and spiritual spectacle that combines the elements of fire, water, and air to honor the river and symbolize gratitude and purification. The word aarti comes from the Sanskrit word ārātrika, which means "the thing that removes the darkness".

Manikarnika Ghat

Considered to be one of the holiest places in the world to die, The Manikarnika Ghat of Varanasi is located between the Scindia Ghat and the Scandia Ghat. It is the most popular cremation ground in Varanasi and witnesses the burning pyres of around 200 to 300 bodies every day. In fact, it is said that it is constantly burning with the fires of pyres. Popular Bollywood movies like ‘Banaras: A Mystic Love Story’ and ‘Kaashi: In search of Ganga’, which showed the spirituality and dharmic beauty of Varanasi were also shot at this famous Ghat.
